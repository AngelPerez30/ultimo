Marmalade
Kiwi







  • Ingredients:

  • 1 kilo of ripe kiwis

  • 500 g of sugar

  • juice of half a large lemon

  • 1 teaspoon agar-agar (optional)

    • Preparation

    Peel the kiwis and cut them coarsely, placing them in a pot or casserole large enough, trying to collect all the juices they release. Water with lemon juice and cover with sugar. Mix well, cover with a cloth and let stand at least two hours, or better still all night.

    Place the casserole in the fire and bring to a boil. Leave to cook over low heat, stirring occasionally. Remove a little foam that may come loose. Meanwhile, prepare the jars. Check that they are intact and with the caps without bumps or oxidation marks. Wash them well, dry them and place them in the oven at about 180ÂºC. Boil the lids in water.

    Check the jam point until the desired thickness is achieved. It should reduce the liquid enough, and the fruit be almost discarded. After about 45 minutes, it will have thickened enough. You can get the most typical jam texture by adding the agar at the end of cooking. Pack very carefully, with the jars still hot, filling them to the brim. Clean the thread, close well and turn upside down. When they cool, the effect will be empty. Store in a dark and cool place.
